The Diagnosis
Beautiful mission. Broken website.

We audited every page of sunrisealaska.org. Here's what we found — and what families, guardians, and care coordinators experience when they visit.

Critical Issues We Found

  • 4 major pages return 404 errors — About Us, Our Team, Day Habilitation, and Supported Employment all show "Page not found." Families clicking your navigation hit dead ends.
  • Extremely thin service content — Group Home page is 3 sentences. Supported Living is a short paragraph and bullet list. Families making life-changing decisions need more information to build trust.
  • No team/staff profiles visible — The Our Team page is broken. Families want to see who will be caring for their loved ones. This is the #1 trust signal in IDD care.
  • No educational resources — Zero blog posts, zero guides, zero FAQ page. Families navigating the waiver process for the first time have nowhere to turn.
  • No testimonials page — One review on the homepage. You need a dedicated page with family stories, guardian feedback, and community endorsements.
  • No waiver education content — You accept IDD, ISW, CCMC, and APDD waivers but don't explain them anywhere. Families don't know what these mean.
  • WordPress + Beaver Builder — Heavy page builder creating slow load times and limiting SEO flexibility. Modern sites load 2-3x faster.
  • No schema markup for services — Basic AIOSEO schema only. Missing LocalBusiness, NonprofitOrganization, and FAQPage structured data that helps Google and AI search engines understand your services.

What You're Doing Right

  • Genuine, heartfelt mission — "Care, Integrity, Empowerment, and Transparency" comes through in your content and the Seward train story
  • "Risers" branding is powerful — Calling residents "Risers" shows respect and empowerment. This should be front and center, not buried.
  • Strong careers page — Signing bonuses, MANDT/ABA training, 401K match. This attracts quality caregivers and should be amplified.
  • Growing operations — 3 homes with a 4th opening in 2026 shows momentum and community trust.
  • Active social media presence — Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn all linked and active.
  • Community events & outings — Seward train trips, YMCA outings, wildlife center visits. This is the kind of content that builds trust with families.
  • Multiple waiver types accepted — IDD, ISW, CCMC, APDD coverage means you serve a wide range of needs.

The New Standard
What families actually need from your website.

We researched IDD care providers nationally and analyzed what the best-in-class organizations provide online. Here's what Sunrise Community Living's website should include.

For Families & Guardians (The Decision Makers)

  • Detailed service pages with real depth — Each service (Group Home, Supported Living, Day Hab, Supported Employment) needs its own comprehensive page explaining what a typical day looks like, staffing ratios, activities, and outcomes.
  • "A Day in the Life" content — Photo galleries and stories showing Risers on outings, cooking, working, and living their best lives. This is what families are really looking for.
  • Staff profiles with photos — Families want to see who's caring for their loved ones. Name, photo, certifications, why they love this work.
  • Waiver guide & enrollment walkthrough — "How to get started" page explaining IDD, ISW, CCMC, and APDD waivers in plain language. Step-by-step enrollment process.
  • FAQ page — "What are visiting hours?", "Can my loved one bring personal items?", "How do you handle medical emergencies?", "What does a typical day look like?"
  • Testimonials & family stories — Dedicated page with guardian feedback, family testimonials, and community endorsements. Video testimonials are 10x more powerful.
  • Virtual tour / photo gallery — Clean, modern photos of each home. Families want to see the living environment before visiting in person.

For Care Coordinators & Referral Partners

  • Professional referral page — Streamlined referral process for care coordinators. List accepted waivers, availability, and intake process clearly.
  • Availability dashboard — Current openings across homes and programs. Care coordinators need this to make placements quickly.
  • Credentialing & compliance info — Licensing, state certifications, staff training standards (MANDT, ABA, ASAM). This builds professional trust.

For Potential Caregivers (Recruitment)

  • Careers hub with culture content — Your signing bonus and training programs are strong. Add employee testimonials, day-in-the-life videos, and career growth paths.
  • Easy online application — Direct application form, not just "contact us." Reduce friction for qualified candidates.
  • Training & development showcase — MANDT, ABA, ASAM, CPR/First Aid. Show that Sunrise invests in its staff.

For Search Engines & AI (Visibility)

  • LocalBusiness + NonprofitOrganization schema — Structured data so Google and AI assistants understand exactly what you do, where you are, and who you serve.
  • FAQPage schema — Your FAQ answers show up directly in Google search results and AI overviews.
  • Blog / resource library — Educational content about IDD care in Alaska: navigating the waiver process, choosing a group home, rights of individuals with disabilities, Alaska-specific resources.
  • AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — When a family asks ChatGPT or Google AI "best group homes for adults with disabilities in Anchorage," Sunrise should be the answer.
  • Dynamic sitemap — Proper XML sitemap with all pages indexed and no 404 errors.
